Fun Fact:


The first computer virus was a Creeper.

Just like a virus infects a human body by replicating versions of itself to pass on to other hosts, a computer virus spreads by inserting its own code and spreading to new computers via networks. The idea of a computer virus was published in the essay “Theory of self-reproducing automata” by John von Neumann in 1949, but the first replicating computer program was not written until 1971. The program was not actively malicious software as it caused no damage to data, the only effect being a message it output to the teletype reading “I’M THE CREEPER; CATCH ME IF YOU CAN”. The virus was not created to bring harm, but it did not take long for the idea of self-replicating software to turn to the dark side. Good news, there are “ethical hackers” out there that work for the good guys. In fact, it’s a great career field!
